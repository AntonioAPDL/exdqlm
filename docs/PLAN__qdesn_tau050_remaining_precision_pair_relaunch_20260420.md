## Promotion / Stop Rule

| Outcome | Next action |
|---|---|
| both succeed | remaining hard-fail surface is fully recovered |
| AL succeeds, EXAL fails | keep AL fixed and investigate an EXAL-only stronger precision kernel |
| EXAL succeeds, AL fails | keep EXAL fixed and inspect whether AL needs stronger conditioning than `1e-6` |
| both fail | stop broad relaunches and do a code-level precision-sampler investigation before more compute |

## Why This Is The Right Next Step

- it preserves the successful run-specific philosophy
- it avoids relaunching any already recovered roots
- it matches each remaining failure to the exact mechanism that still appears active
- it is small, auditable, and easy to interpret
